# IPL Analysis Dashboard (2008 - 2025)

## Description
An interactive data visualization dashboard designed to analyze historical and seasonal insights of the Indian Premier League (IPL) from 2008 to 2025. The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of team performance, player statistics, and match trends using dynamic visuals and filters for better decision-making and exploration.
<br><br>

## Project Overview
### 1. Objective:
- To develop an interactive dashboard that provides clear and actionable insights into Indian Premier League data by visualizing team performance, player statistics, and season trends.
<br><br>
### 2. Problem Statement
- The data of the Indian Premier League is extensive and distributed across multiple sources, making it difficult to quickly extract meaningful insights. Traditional formats such as raw datasets and static tables fail to effectively highlight trends, comparisons, and performance metrics of teams and players. As a result, analysts and users struggle to make informed decisions or understand season-wise patterns. Therefore, there is a need for a centralized, interactive dashboard that transforms complex IPL data into clear, visual, and easily interpretable insights.
<br><br>
### 3. Tools:
- Power BI

### 2. Data Cleaning and Transformation
- The dataset for the Indian Premier League was preprocessed to ensure accuracy, consistency, and usability for analysis. Missing values were handled by either imputing relevant data or removing incomplete records where necessary. Duplicate entries were identified and eliminated to maintain data integrity. Data types were standardized (e.g., dates, numerical fields), and columns were renamed for clarity and consistency.
- Additionally, transformations were performed such as creating new calculated fields (e.g., strike rate, total runs, wickets, boundaries), aggregating match-level data into season-level insights, and categorizing players based on roles (batter, bowler, all-rounder). The cleaned and transformed dataset enabled efficient visualization and accurate insights in the dashboard.
<br><br>
### 3. Data Analysis
- The cleaned dataset of the Indian Premier League was analyzed to uncover meaningful patterns and performance insights across seasons. Key metrics such as total runs, wickets, fours, sixes, and match outcomes were evaluated to identify top-performing players and teams. Season-wise analysis helped determine champions, runner-ups, and overall team consistency.
- Comparative analysis was performed to highlight leading run scorers, highest wicket-takers, and players with the most boundaries. Team performance was assessed using points tables and win-loss trends, while player-level analysis categorized individuals based on their roles and contributions. These insights were then used to create interactive visualizations, enabling users to explore trends, compare performances, and make data-driven interpretations easily.
<br><br>

## Target Audience
The dashboard is designed for a wide range of users interested in the Indian Premier League, including cricket analysts, sports enthusiasts, team strategists, and data analysts. It is also useful for students and professionals learning data visualization and analytics, as well as fans who want to explore player statistics, team performance, and season trends in an interactive and easy-to-understand format.
<br><br>

## Key Features
### KPIs
### 1. Overview Page

Primary KPIs
- Total Matches Played
- Total Teams
- Total Runs / Boundaries (Fours & Sixes)
- Champions & Runner-Up (Season-wise)
- Top Run Scorer
- Top Wicket-Taker

Secondary KPIs
- Total Centuries & Half-Centuries
- Total Venues
- Most Fours by Player
- Most Sixes by Player
- Season-wise Points Table
- Year Filter for trend analysis

### 2. Team Page

Primary KPIs
- Team Titles Won
- Total Matches Played by Team
- Win Percentage
- Current Captain & Coach

Secondary KPIs
- Team Owner & Home Venue
- Season-wise Performance
- Team Squad Overview
- Historical Achievements

### 3. Player Page

Primary KPIs
- Total Runs / Wickets (based on role)
- Batting Strike Rate / Bowling Economy
- Matches Played
- Player Role (Batter, Bowler, All-rounder, Wicketkeeper)

Secondary KPIs
- Total Fours & Sixes
- Batting Average / Bowling Average
- Player Comparison within Team
- Squad Distribution by Role

## Data Visualization
The dashboard presents insights from the Indian Premier League using clear, interactive, and visually engaging elements. Key metrics are highlighted through KPI cards, while bar charts and column charts are used to compare team and player performances such as runs, wickets, and boundaries. Line charts help visualize season-wise trends, and tables display detailed information like points standings and player statistics.

Interactive filters and slicers (e.g., season, team) allow users to explore the data dynamically and focus on specific insights. Consistent color themes, icons, and layout design enhance readability and user experience, making complex cricket data easy to understand and analyze.
